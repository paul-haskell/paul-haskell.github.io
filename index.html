Personal Trivia

Stories

Paul earned his Bachelor's, Master's, and Ph.D. degrees in Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences at the University of California, Berkeley, specializing in video processing, compression, and transmission. He received a research fellowship from the National Science Foundation and also was granted the Berkeley Fellowship for graduate study. As a undergraduate, he received the Departmental Citation as the top graduating student in his department.

After university, Paul worked at several leading-edge companies that developed video products for the entertainment television industry. His final position was as a Vice President of R&D for Harmonic Inc, where he and his team achieved several firsts:

  • First variable bit-rate HEVC video coder
  • First variable bit-rate AVC video coder
  • First wide-area variable bit-rate video coder

Paul and his team received several Technical Emmy awards for their technologies. Paul holds 13 patents for video compression and transmission inventions.

Paul currently is a Term Assistant Professor of Computer Science at the University of San Francisco, where he teaches several undergraduate courses.
